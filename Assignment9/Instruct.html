Pick Your First Guitar Quiz

Step 1: Tell me whatcha want!

What level of cool do you want to be? Taylor Swift Acoustic? Flea Bass? Jimi Hendrix Electric? All are fine, some are harder to learn on than others.

Step 2: "I'm not here to make sense, I'm here to make dollars!"

You go into debt after a medical emergency, not after buying a guitar. Buy according to your budget and needs.

Step 3: You actually do know what you get in a box of chocolates, but you should try each one for Forrest Gump's sake.

I know going outside and talking to people is hard, but buying a guitar without feeling the guitar robs you of a cool 'main character' moment of buying a guitar in store. Walk into the store, it'll be cool.

Step 4: A snuggie for your hands! Or not, especially if you enjoy mild discomfort.

Guitars are so many different shapes and sizes! Andre the Giant's guitar size is probably different than yours. Pick one that fits you.

Step 5: Not all guitars are made equal! I made a guitar out of a cereal box once.

If you need an instruction manual on how to build a guitar out of a cereal bar, find a box of fruit loops from, like, 2010. Make sure you pick a guitar with good quality materials.

Step 6: Remember in step 1, where I said some are harder to learn than others? This is that. Electric guitars and bass guitars are harder to learn, you'll need to learn about the electronics it uses.

If you’re buying an electric guitar, check its pickups and controls.

Step 7: You've got 99 problems and keeping track of accessories is at least 5 of them.

Don’t forget about accessories like strings, a tuner, and a case.

Step 8: You need an Obi Wan Kenobi! All good adventurers have a good moment where they meet a wise mentor. Don't skip out on advice.

Get recommendations from experienced players or staff in a music store.

Types of Sounding Guitars

Pick Your First Guitar Quiz

What style of music do you want to play?

You might want to try an electric guitar, like a Fender Stratocaster for Rock/Metal.

Consider a semi-hollow guitar, like an Epiphone Dot for Blues/Jazz.

Try an acoustic guitar like a Yamaha FG800 for Acoustic/Folk music.

What’s your budget?

You can get a quality beginner guitar like a Squier Stratocaster within this budget.

Consider a mid-range guitar like an Epiphone Les Paul Standard for this budget.

Check out premium models like a Fender American Professional Stratocaster.

Do you want a smaller, beginner-friendly guitar?

A smaller guitar like a Fender CP-60S Parlor would be a good choice for comfort.

Go for a full-size guitar like a Seagull S6 or a Yamaha FG800.
